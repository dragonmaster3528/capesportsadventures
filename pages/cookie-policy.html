Cookie Policy

Effective Date: 1 January 2026

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, understand how you use the site, and improve your experience. Some cookies are essential for the website to function; others help us analyse traffic or remember your settings.

How We Use Cookies

Cape Sports Adventures uses cookies to:

  • Keep the website functioning properly
  • Remember your itinerary selections as you browse
  • Save your accessibility preferences (text size, contrast, motion settings)
  • Understand how visitors use our site so we can improve it
  • Measure the effectiveness of our marketing efforts

Types of Cookies We Use

These cookies are necessary for the website to function. They cannot be disabled.

Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website. All data is anonymised.

Marketing Cookies

These cookies may be set by our advertising partners to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant ads on other sites. We currently do not use marketing cookies, but this may change in the future. This policy will be updated accordingly.

Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are placed by third-party services that appear on our pages:

  • Google Analytics: Web analytics service (Privacy Policy)
  • Google Fonts: Web font delivery (Privacy Policy)

Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies from specific sites
  • Block all cookies entirely
  • Delete all cookies when you close the browser

Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Note: Blocking essential cookies may prevent some features of our website from working properly, such as the itinerary builder and accessibility settings.

Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that signals to websites that you don't want your online activity tracked. Our website currently does not respond to Do Not Track signals, but you can opt out of analytics tracking by using browser extensions or adjusting your cookie settings.
